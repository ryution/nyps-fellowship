Inspired Minds

What is virtue? What does a life well-lived actually require?

The Academy Fellowship is not a lecture series. It is a demanding, six-week intellectual community built on the conviction that the most important questions are not answered in a classroom — they are wrestled with, together, by people who refuse to take easy answers.

Fellows read through the Great Books across traditions — Greek, Chinese, Indian, Hebrew — and meet weekly in extended Socratic seminar. They are expected to ask not only what is true, but what such truths demand of us.

No prior philosophy background required. Only curiosity, intellectual honesty, and the willingness to have your thinking genuinely challenged.

About the Fellowship

The Academy Fellowship is a six-week philosophy program for exceptional high school students run by the New York Philosophical Society. Fellows read through the Great Books, meet weekly in extended seminar, and test their ideas through close dialogue with faculty and peers.

The program takes place in Midtown Manhattan from July 13 through August 23, 2026. Fellows meet one to two times per week in person for extended seminar and are expected to complete 10–15 hours of independent reading to prepare for each session.

The program is entirely free of charge. Students with financial need receive a stipend to cover materials and transportation.

Vision & Mission

We believe philosophy is not a subject. It is a practice — the oldest and most urgent one we have. Our mission is to recover that practice in the life of young New Yorkers, and to build a community where the examined life is not an aspiration but a living reality.

The New York Philosophical Society was founded on the conviction that meaning and human connection can be restored through the shared pursuit of wisdom. The Academy Fellowship is the most intensive expression of that conviction.

Our Program

The Academy Fellowship emphasizes an open, rigorous, and collaborative approach to learning. The program is designed around the Socratic method: close reading, sustained dialogue, and the willingness to follow an argument wherever it leads.

Unlike most academic enrichment programs, the Fellowship assigns no grades and offers no college credit. Fellows are trusted to set their own limits and to pursue understanding for its own sake.

Each week, fellows complete substantial reading from the core curriculum independently, then gather for an extended seminar of two to three hours. Sessions are led by faculty but shaped by fellows — the best conversations arise from genuine disagreement.

Core Reading

The curriculum spans the great philosophical traditions of the ancient world. Fellows read primary texts in their entirety — not excerpts or summaries.

Seminar Structure

Fellows meet one to two times per week in Midtown Manhattan for extended seminars of two to three hours. Between sessions, fellows are expected to complete 10–15 hours of independent reading.

Seminars are small by design — never more than 15 fellows at a time — to ensure that every voice is heard and every idea genuinely tested. Faculty do not lecture; they ask questions.

The program concludes with a Capstone Dialogue in which each fellow presents and defends an original philosophical position before the full cohort and faculty panel.

Applications for the Summer 2026 Academy Fellowship open March 15, 2026 and close May 20, 2026. The application consists of two short essays, an official transcript, and a brief interview conducted by members of our admissions committee.

First-timePriority given to first-time Academy Fellowship applicants

Required Components

All applicants must submit the following:

  • Two short essays (prompts provided in application)
  • Official high school transcript
  • Brief interview with admissions committee (by invitation)

No letters of recommendation are required. No application fee.

Tuition & Aid

$0

The Academy Fellowship is entirely free of tuition. We believe cost should never be a barrier to the life of the mind. There are no program fees, no application fees, and no hidden costs.

Need-Based Financial Aid

Students with demonstrated financial need will receive a stipend to cover the cost of required course materials, including physical copies of texts on the reading list.

Financial need is determined through a brief, confidential section of the application. There is no threshold — all applicants are encouraged to apply for aid if they have any financial need.

Transportation Stipend

Fellows who require financial assistance with transportation to and from weekly seminars in Midtown Manhattan are eligible to receive a transportation stipend for the duration of the program.

To apply for the transportation stipend, indicate your need in the financial aid section of the application. Stipend amounts are determined on a case-by-case basis.

For questions about financial aid or stipends, contact us at fellowship@nyphilosophy.org.

FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

What are you looking for in applicants?+
We are looking for students who are genuinely curious, introspective, and willing to have their thinking challenged. Academic achievement matters, but we are equally interested in how you think and what you care about. There is no ideal background or profile.
Do I need prior philosophy experience?+
No prior philosophy background is required or expected. Many of our strongest fellows have never taken a philosophy class. What matters is your willingness to read carefully and engage honestly.
How do I apply?+
Applications open March 15, 2026 and close May 20, 2026. The application consists of two short essays, an official transcript, and a brief interview conducted by members of our admissions committee. There is no application fee.
Who is eligible?+
The Academy Fellowship is open to rising juniors, rising seniors, and incoming college freshmen. Applicants must be at least 16 years old by June 20, 2026 and may not turn 19 before July 31, 2026.
What does it cost?+
The program is entirely free of charge. Students with financial need will receive a stipend to cover materials and transportation.
How often do we meet in person?+
Fellows meet 1–2 times per week in person for extended seminar in Midtown Manhattan and are expected to complete 10–15 hours of independent reading to prepare for each session.
Is the program residential?+
The program is a day program. Fellows commute to Midtown Manhattan for weekly seminars. Transportation stipends are available for those with demonstrated financial need.
Are grades or college credit offered?+
No grades are assigned and no college credit is offered. Fellows receive a certificate of completion from the New York Philosophical Society at the end of the program.
